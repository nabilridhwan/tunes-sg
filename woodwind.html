Flute

A flute is an aerophone or reedless wind instrument that produces its sound from the flow of air across an opening. It has a clear and bright sound with a distinctive warmth, refinement, and subtlety to its tone.

Clarinet

Clarinet has a single-reed mouthpiece, a straight, cylindrical tube with an almost cylindrical bore, and a flared bell. It also boasts a register of approximately four octaves-the largest of any wind instrument.

Saxaphone

The saxophone is usually made of brass and played with a single-reed mouthpiece. It is an instrument with a tone somewhere between a brass instrument and a woodwind. The sax is a must in all types of music from jazz, to classical, to pop, when a smooth and bright sound is called for.

Oboe

The oboe is a type of double reed woodwind instrument and the most common oboe plays in the treble or soprano range. Its name comes from the French for "high-pitched wood," are excellent for solo performances with many notes in high ranges. The oboe is extremely difficult to play.

Bassoon

The bassoon is a woodwind instrument in the double reed family that plays music written in the bass and tenor clefs, and occasionally the treble. The long, wide wooden pipe of the bassoon contains much wisdom.

Recorder

Recorder known as internal duct flutes—flutes with a whistle mouthpiece, also known as fipple flutes. It is also familiar to most as the instrument used today in school lessons. However, its history extends to ancient times, and modern day performers are active in a wide range of settings.
